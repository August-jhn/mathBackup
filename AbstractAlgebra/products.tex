Group products:\\

We have two notions. The external direct product, denoted \\
$G_1\oplus G_2\oplus\dots\oplus G_k = \overline{G}\cong G_1G_2...G_k$, where the last thing is called the internal direct product. The first is like mulitplication, the second is like factoring.\\

GIven a finite collection $(G_1,+_1), (G_2,+_2),\dots,(G_k,+_k)$. The external direct product, $G_1\oplus G_2\oplus\dots\oplus G_k = \{(g_1,g_2,\dots,g_l ): g_i\in G_i \forall i\le i\le k)\}$ is a group whose operation is comopnentwise determined by the operations of the component groups.\\
