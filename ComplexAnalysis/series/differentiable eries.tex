\thm Let $C$ be a contour interior to the C of C of $S(z) = \infsum a_n(z-z_0)^n$ and let $g$ be continuoius on $C$. Then $\int_Cg(t)S(z)dz = \infsum a_n\int_C(g())$\\

\defn A singular point $z_0$ is called isolated if there exists some $\epsilon > 0$ such that $f$ is analytic on $0<|z-z_0|<\epsilon$. \\

Since $f$ is analytic on this disk, it has a laurant series on $0<|z-z_0| < \epsilon$. This will look like $f(z) = \infsum a_n(z-z_0)^n + \infsum \frac{b_n}{(z-z_0)^n}$. Where $b_n = \frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_C\frac{f(z)}{(z-z_0)^{-n+1}}dz$. If $n = 1$ we have $2\pi i b_1 = \int_Cf(z)dz$.\\

\defn The residue of $f$ at the isolated singular point $z_0$ is residue $ Resf(z) = b_n$.
